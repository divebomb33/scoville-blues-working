Scoville Blues

Who are Scoville Blues?

The Scoville Blues Band is a high energy blues band with many different influences to help define their style. From contemporary blues rock to traditional Texas and Chicago styles to funky New Orleans blues the band has something for everybody.

Based out of Bucks County, Pennsylvania the band has been playing bars, restaurants and festivals in the Philadelphia / Tri-State area for over four years. Along the way they've played the Lehigh River Blues Jam in 2008 and 2009, the Bensalem Fall Festival, the Olde Kings Blues Jam in Swedesboro, NJ, the Valenzano Wine Festival in Shamong, NJ, the Vietnam Vets Memorial Festival in Falls Township, PA, the Burlington Jazz & Blues Heritage Festival in Burlington, NJ, and the benefits for bluesmen Lonnie Shields and Georgie Bonds.

Other highlights include opening up for rock legend Dave Mason at the Scottish Rite Auditorium in Collingswood, NJ in November of 2008 and opening up for Chris Cain at the Hill Theatre in Paulsboro, NJ in May of 2008. On June 28, 2009 the band played at the Fourth Annual "Taste of Philadelphia Festival" at Penns Landing in Philadelphia, PA, and then on April 28, 2010 they were back at the Scottish Rite Auditorium to open for blues great Walter Trout. More recently Scoville Blues played at the State Street Blues Stroll in Media, PA on June 12, 2010 and the Riverfront Blues Festival in Wilmington, DE on August 7, 2010, at Natali Vineyards on January 22, 2011, the Paoli Blues Festival on October 1, 2011 and the Anglesea Blues Festival on July 14, 2012.

This five piece band features [updates coming soon] Dave Piersig on lead vocals and percussion, on lead guitar and vocals, on bass, Jim Bowman on drums and percussion and Dave "Bags" Badgley on harp, sax and vocals. With scorching guitar, dynamic harmonica, passionate vocals and a driving rhythm section Scoville Blues are a band you shouldn't miss!

What is a Scoville?

The Scoville scale is a measure of the hotness or piquancy of a chili pepper, as defined by the amount of capsaicin, ( A chemical compound which stimulates nerve endings in the skin ) present.

Meet the Band

Dave
Piersig

Lead Vox/Percussion

The Voice of Scoville Blues is the story teller of some of the most classic blues arrangement's and brings to life the stories of the blues. Piersig's strong tenor voice powers the emotional expression of the songs he sings. For almost a decade Piersig has written and arranged his original songs for Scoville Blues to make the first impression a lasting one. His background as a drummer/percussionists makes his timing and rhythmic changes tight and classic in its presentation. He has been called a blues crooner by the Media press. Quoted as something different in his vocal expression by Bucks County News. Writting his first song in 2008 “Your Not The Same” it was heard on You Tube and gained international recognition when added to a digital album Absolute NY Blues... In the time Piersig and Scoville has performed in the tri state bars clubs festivals and theaters they have played with some of the greats from Dave Mason to Canned Heat, Walter rout and other greats. His blues influences are from the greats in music but his style is all his own. Piersigs ability to grow and learn from his mentors in the music industry continues the growth of his vocal popularity. Charlie
Cappello

Lead Guitar/Vox

Chalie "Clean Hands" is a true old soul in the blues. His background and knowledge of blues history rounds out the talent he unleashes at each performance. His guitar is the conduit for the highest energy of red hot solos. Back in the day his time playing with Chubby Checker was a launching pad for the future of his success. Traveling and performing on the east coast he was developing his own class of guitar work. Chalie has many influences from the blues greats and has created his own true signature sound that brings a raw yet clean dynamic sound to the blues.

Bill
Hubbard

Bass Guitar

Born and raised in Washington, DC, Bill came by his love of the Blues in an unusual way. When he was 10, he asked his Mother to buy him a “Blues” album based on his early exposure to Cream and Hendrix. She came home with a copy of Albert King’s “Live Wire Blues Power” and that was all it took for him to start his life long love of the Blues.He started like many do playing the guitar, a Christmas gift when he was 14. He transitioned to the bass and was in a very successful band in High School named Brew that had a great following in the Washington DC metro area, including regular gigs at Fraternity parties at American University and the University of Maryland.Once out of school, Bill set out on a career and put the bass down for many years. On his 50th birthday, he made the decision to start doing things he liked for a change and bought another bass as a Birthday present to himself.The passion was reignited and he has hasn’t put it down since.He worked his way back up throughout the local ranks, running Blues Jams and playing in many bands in the Lehigh Valley and Philadelphia area, including Ball and Chain, The Blues Factor, Blue Waters and fill ins with The Mississippi Pig Farmers. Bill joined Scoville Blues in 2013 and is thrilled to be playing with a great, high energy group of some of the best Blues musicians in the Tri State area.

Dave
Badgley

Harp/Sax/Vocals

Dave was born in Philadelphia. His father, a big band trumpet player, was rated in the top 10 of "Downbeat" magazine 8 years in a row due to the company he kept: Gene Krupa at 17 years old, to Buddy Rich, Frank Sinatra, Mel Torme and all of the heavy hitters of the era. David started on clarinet in school, picked up the harmonica and guitar in high school and moved to saxophone in college. Dave has been in an active band since the early 80's concentrating primarily on the Blues. Married with four children, he resides in Langhorne, PA.

Jim
Bowman

Drums/Percussion

Studying piano, vocals, drums and various percussion, Jim has played everything from punk to funk in cover, original and tribute acts. Highlights from singing years include performances at the Academy of Music, Veterans Stadium, and an East to West coast tour of Canada. Highlights across 36+ years of traditional, hand and electronic percussion include opening for majors such as Tower of Power, Spyro Gyra, Dave Mason, Walter Trout and World Party.
In 2015, Jim was chosen to represent the local Blues Foundation as winner of a regional Blues Foundation International Blues Challenge. Jim travelled to Memphis to compete at an international level, and can’t wait to return!
Jim has contributed to at least 5 commercial music releases, some receiving international acclaim and local radio play.
Jim continues to focus on drums, vocals and percussion, but has also recently taken up interests in sound engineering and lighting as the resident sound and light man for band.
When not drumming, Jim spends his time engrossed in kid events and tinkering with all things tech.
